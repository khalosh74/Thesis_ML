# Use and Misuse Boundaries

## Intended use

This framework is intended for:

- leakage-aware scientific experimentation.
- locked comparison evaluation.
- confirmatory protocol execution under frozen thesis contracts.
- reproducibility and artifact-audit workflows.

## Non-intended use

This framework is not intended for:

- clinical diagnosis, treatment selection, or patient-level decision support.
- causal inference from predictive performance.
- anatomical localization claims from linear coefficients alone.
- unrestricted external population generalization claims from internal-only data.
- "emotion reading" claims beyond the locked target/scope definitions.

## Evidence boundaries by mode

- Exploratory mode:
  - idea generation and debugging only.
  - outputs are not confirmatory evidence.
- Locked comparison mode:
  - method-lock comparisons within declared spec boundaries.
  - supports model-selection decisions inside the registered comparison scope.
- Confirmatory mode:
  - final thesis evidence under frozen protocol conditions.
  - constrained by claim IDs, interpretation limits, multiplicity, controls, and
    deviation governance.

## Subgroup, transfer, and interpretability boundaries

- Subgroup results are descriptive unless explicitly promoted by locked policy.
- Subgroup findings cannot substitute for primary confirmatory evidence.
- Transfer analyses are secondary unless explicitly locked as primary.
- Coefficient-based interpretability is model-behavior support, not direct biological
  localization evidence.

## External validation boundary

In this phase, external validation is compatibility-only. Compatibility reports are
governance checks and must not be presented as external efficacy/performance evidence.

## Confirmatory-ready warning

"Confirmatory-ready" means the run/release satisfies governance and scientific contract
checks. It does not mean the system is deployable or approved for real-world decision-making.
